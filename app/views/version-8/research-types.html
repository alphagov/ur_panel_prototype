Tell me more about the different types of research

There are different types of research that you can get involved in.

If you do in-person research with us (either at a research lab or in your home), you'll receive compensation to cover your time and any travel expenses. We usually give you vouchers as compensation.

Visit a government research lab

You’ll be invited to take part in a face-to-face session at research lab near you. We’ll ask you to try new services and talk to our researchers about how you’ve used government services in the past. These sessions usually last less than an hour. You'll receive compensation (usually vouchers) to cover your time.

We'll visit you

Our researchers will visit you at home or work to ask you questions about how you use GOV.UK. These interviews usually last under 2 hours. You'll receive compensation (usually vouchers) to cover your time.

Online sessions

During online sessions, you’ll be given a task to complete on GOV.UK. You don’t need to travel anywhere for these sessions but you’ll need your own computer, phone or tablet to participate. These sessions usually last 30 minutes. We don’t usually give you any compensation for this.

Will I get paid?

We usually give you vouchers if you take part in research in-person. We don’t usually give you vouchers if you take part in online research. When we ask you to participate in research, we’ll tell you about any payment you could get. We’re not able to offer cash payments.
